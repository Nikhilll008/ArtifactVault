About the Maharashtra Heritage Museum

Two decades of documenting, conserving and sharing the material history of the Maratha Empire and the Deccan plateau.

Home / About Museum
Our History

From a District Archive to a Digital Museum

2003

Founding Collection

The museum began as a district-level repository for artifacts recovered during conservation surveys of Raigad, Pratapgad and Sindhudurg forts, housed initially within the Pune district archive.

2009

Permanent Gallery Opens

A permanent public gallery opened in Shivajinagar, Pune, bringing together Maratha weaponry, coinage and fort models under one roof for the first time.

2016

Manuscript & Textile Wing

A dedicated wing for Modi-script manuscripts and traditional Maharashtrian textiles was added in partnership with regional weaving cooperatives and private collectors.

2022

Digitisation Programme

The museum began systematically photographing, measuring and recording every item in its collection, laying the groundwork for a digital catalog.

2026

ArtifactVault Launches

ArtifactVault goes live as the museum's official digital catalog and heritage item management system, supporting both public exploration and internal curatorial workflows.

Our Purpose

Mission & Vision

Mission

To identify, conserve and document the tangible heritage of Maharashtra — its forts, weapons, currency, sculpture, manuscripts and textiles — and to make this knowledge freely accessible to students, researchers and the public through accurate digital cataloguing.

Vision

To be the most reliable digital reference for Maharashtra's Maratha-era and pre-Maratha heritage, supporting education, academic research and responsible inter-institutional loan of cultural artifacts across the state.

Conservation & Outreach

Heritage Preservation Initiatives

  • Digital Documentation

    High-resolution photography and metadata recording for every catalogued artifact, forming the basis of ArtifactVault.

  • Conservation Laboratory

    On-site treatment of metal corrosion, textile fragility and manuscript degradation by trained conservators.

  • Educational Outreach

    Guided tours and curated worksheets for schools and colleges, linking museum holdings to the Maharashtra history syllabus.

  • Community Loan Programme

    Responsible short-term loans of artifacts to partner museums, universities and cultural institutions, tracked through ArtifactVault's loan module.
